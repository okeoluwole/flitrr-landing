0. The rule this sheet enforces
Nothing on this surface is generated. Every string is selected by a rule from the engine's output. A state that can occur and has no string here is a gap in this sheet, never a licence to invent one.
This sheet covers Band 3, the attention list, and the workspace tile that opens the finished surface. Bands 1 and 2 are shipped and their copy sheet stands.
________________________________________
1. Band 3: What needs you now
1.1 The band heading
What needs you now
1.2 The empty state
When nothing is flagged. One line, and then stop.
Nothing needs you right now. Every flagged item is being handled.
No list, no filler beneath it. Proportional monitoring is silent when things are fine, exactly as the Action Log's own calm line already is.
1.3 The row
Each row is one underlying thing, after dedupe. It carries:
•	What it is, in plain words. The risk's description, the action's description, the milestone or gate name.
•	Which objective it serves, as a quiet tag. Except the gate, which serves all five and is tagged accordingly. See 1.5.
•	Why it is here, the trigger that flagged it, in a few words.
•	Where it goes. The whole row deep-links to the item in its home module.
1.4 The "why it is here" line
Selected by the flag that put the item on the list. Every trigger the sources can emit must appear here.
From the risk monitor, the four triggers already built:
Trigger	Line
escalated-severity	Its severity has risen.
critical-and-unmanaged	Critical, and nothing is being done yet.
went-stale	Overdue for review.
not-yet-engaged	Critical, and not yet looked at.
From the Action Log:
Source	Line
Open critical action	A critical action, still open.
Needs your response	Waiting on your response.
From the Programme:
Source	Line
Milestone flagged red	A milestone serving [objective] has slipped past your tolerance.
Milestone flagged amber	A milestone serving [objective] is slipping.
Gate flagged	See 1.5.
1.5 The gate row
A slipping gate sorts above everything, because it answers to all five objectives rather than one.
Its tag is not an objective. It reads:
Gate
Its line, by flag:
Red:
The gate into Stage [N] has slipped past your tolerance.
Amber:
The gate into Stage [N] is slipping.
It links to the Programme, since no single module owns a gate.
1.6 The dedupe, rendered
Where a risk and its promoted action would both appear, one row shows, and it is the action. The risk is named on it.
Mitigate: gaps or overlaps between consultant scopes Cost. A critical action, still open. Raised from a Serious risk.
This is the M7.2 rule, reused. The list must never show a risk and its own tracked action as two rows.
1.7 The footer
When the list is capped and more remains:
[N] more need your attention.
It links to wherever the largest share of the overflow sits. Where the overflow spans several modules, it links to the module holding the most items, and the count is the true total across all of them.

2. The workspace tile
The tile that has read "Coming soon" since M9.2. It now opens the finished surface.
2.1 The tile, once the surface is live
Heading:
Project dashboard
Line:
Where the project stands against the objectives you set.
It carries a single live signal, and only one, so the workspace does not become a second dashboard. The project state, in words, no colour:
Green:
Every objective holding.
Amber:
Under pressure.
Red:
A protected objective is compromised.
No state:
Nothing scored yet.
No counts on this tile. Every number lives on the dashboard, deduplicated, one source of truth.
2.2 Before the Brief is locked
The tile does not open. It reads:
Lock your Brief to open the dashboard.
